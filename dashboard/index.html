The Strategic Pivot

Nexus Consumer Brands recently deployed an expansive marketing initiative of ₹100 Crore across five vital channels. Relying on legacy "Last-Click" attribution models presents a profound Attribution Blind Spot. This primitive approach misattributes system value by completely ignoring the stochastic, non-linear multi-channel paths modern consumers navigate, leading to severe resource misallocation.

Project ROI Lens eliminates guesswork by sanitizing fraud, mapping mathematical human pathways via a Markov Chain probabilistic framework, quantifying ad fatigue curves, and executing constrained optimization to maximize total conversions.

Slide 03

Attribution Methodology: Multi-Touch Probabilistic Engine

High-level architectural visualization of Markov Chain state transitions.

Markov Chain Transition Engine

Rather than crediting only the last touchpoint, the system maps consumer behavior into a discrete-time Markov chain where each channel represents a state. The true value of a channel is derived via its Removal Effect Index (REI).

By simulating the removal of channel k from the transition network, the new diminished system conversion probability is calculated. The normalized drop reveals the exact weight of fractional conversions that must be credited to that specific channel.

Pij = Σ (Transitions from i to j) / Σ (Total Transitions out of i)

The Fatigue Curve & Diminishing Returns Analysis

Modeling ad fatigue using a saturating logarithmic curve: f(x) = α · ln(x + 1).

Diminishing Returns (Conversions vs Spend)

Ad Fatigue & Logarithmic Saturation

Assuming a linear connection between spend and acquisition yields highly flawed strategies. Every digital channel suffers from diminishing returns due to audience fatigue.

The conversion yield function f(x) for a given budget allocation x is modeled using a saturating logarithmic curve. The fitted parameter α (Alpha) represents the marginal scaling capacity of the channel. The chart visualizes how conversions plateau as spend increases, illustrating where ad networks start wasting money.

Slide 08

Strategic Action Items for the CMO

Clear, prescriptive mandates regarding caps, defunding, and tactical re-routing.

01

Defund Bot Traffic Loops

Immediately implement frequency caps and block IPs associated with rapid-fire click bursts (dwell times of exactly 0 seconds without impression layers). Defunding these simulated phantom conversions saves up to Rs. 1 Crore in wasted ad spend.

02

Implement Channel Caps

Enforce strict programmatic boundaries including an exact ₹5 Crore cap per channel for individual brands. This prevents oversaturating Instagram and Google Search, steering budgets clear of steep diminishing return thresholds.

03

Reallocate to High-Yield Primers

Shift budget out of fatiguing closers (e.g. overfunded Marketplaces) and into high-marginal value Top-of-Funnel primers (e.g. YouTube and Influencers). This synergistic rebalancing secures a projected +323% gain in systemic conversions.
