# Project ROI Lens: CMO Presentation Script & Canva Layout Guide

This document provides a slide-by-slide script, visual design layout instructions, and speaker notes to help you construct a professional, high-impact Canva deck for the Chief Marketing Officer of Nexus Consumer Brands.

## Slide 1: Title & The Attribution Blind Spot
* **Canva Slide Title**: Project ROI Lens: Advanced Marketing Attribution & Budget Refinement
* **Subtitle**: Moving from Legacy Last-Click Models to Probabilistic Customer Journeys
* **Visual Layout Idea**: Deep dark background (e.g. Navy or Charcoal) with a glowing tech graphic (network nodes or flowcharts) representing multi-channel paths.
* **Key Bullet Points**:
  * **The Challenge**: Nexus Consumer Brands recently deployed a ₹100 Crore marketing initiative across 5 vital channels.
  * **The Blind Spot**: Legacy "Last-Click" attribution ignores non-linear user journeys and multi-channel synergies.
  * **The Solution**: Probabilistic attribution sanitizes fraud, maps mathematical paths, and optimizes budget allocations.
* **Speaker Notes**:
  > *"Good morning, team. Today we are presenting Project ROI Lens. Nexus recently spent ₹100 Crore across our channels. However, by relying on legacy Last-Click attribution, we are operating in a blind spot—failing to see how channels work together. ROI Lens uses Markov Chains to map exact customer journeys and optimize future budget allocations."*

## Slide 2: The Auditing Layer (Fraud Prevention)
* **Canva Slide Title**: The Auditing Layer: Defunding Bot Traffic
* **Visual Layout Idea**: A split screen: on the left, a clean Doughnut Chart showing "95.0% Clean Users" vs "5.0% Bots". On the right, a highlighted metric box reading **"₹1.00 Crore Capital Saved"** in bold emerald green text.
* **Key Bullet Points**:
  * **Sanitization**: Filtered non-human traffic using two behavioral delta audits:
    1. *Rapid-fire clicks*: consecutive clicks by a single user with $< 1\,\text{ms}$ time delta.
    2. *Zero-dwell clicks*: clicks with a duration of $0$ seconds without impressions.
  * **Audit Statistics**: Flagged **500 bot profiles** and filtered **5,081 bot clicks**.
  * **Capital Reclaimed**: Saves **₹1.00 Crore** in marketing waste next quarter.
* **Speaker Notes**:
  > *"First, we audited the data for fraud. By filtering users exhibiting sub-millisecond click deltas and zero-second dwell times, we identified that 5% of our traffic consisted of bots. By defunding these bot channels, we immediately reclaim ₹1 Crore in wasted capital."*

## Slide 3: Multi-Touch Attribution Methodology
* **Canva Slide Title**: Attribution Methodology: Absorbing Markov Chains
* **Visual Layout Idea**: A grid matrix or heatmap diagram representing state transition probabilities (e.g. users moving from Instagram to E-commerce Marketplaces).
* **Key Bullet Points**:
  * **Markov Modeling**: Represents user behavior as directional transitions between marketing channels.
  * **Removal Effect Index (REI)**: Simulates removing a single channel and measures the drop in conversion probability.
  * **Matrix Algebra**: Computes the fundamental matrix $N = (I - Q)^{-1}$ to distribute conversion credits fairly.
* **Speaker Notes**:
  > *"To calculate the true value of each channel, we deployed an absorbing Markov Chain. We map the probabilities of users moving from one state to another. By mathematically simulating the removal of a single channel, we calculate its Removal Effect Index. This distributes conversions based on real value, not just the last click."*

## Slide 4: Funnel Classification (Primers vs. Closers)
* **Canva Slide Title**: Funnel Deep-Dive: Role-Based Classification
* **Visual Layout Idea**: A horizontal bar chart or quadrant grid separating **Primers (Top of Funnel)** on the left and **Closers (Bottom of Funnel)** on the right.
* **Key Bullet Points**:
  * **Funnel Classification**: Separates early engagement catalysts from transactional closers.
  * **Primers (awareness)**: *YouTube* and *Influencer Networks* show high Top-of-Funnel weights.
  * **Closers (action)**: *E-commerce Marketplaces* and *Instagram* show high final purchase weights.
* **Speaker Notes**:
  > *"We classified our channels based on their structural role. YouTube and Influencers serve as 'Primers'—driving top-of-funnel awareness. E-commerce Marketplaces and Instagram serve as 'Closers'—capturing the final transaction. Both roles are vital, and we must budget for both."*

## Slide 6: Ad Fatigue & Diminishing Returns
* **Canva Slide Title**: The Fatigue Curve: Diminishing Marginal ROI
* **Visual Layout Idea**: A line graph plotting saturating logarithmic curves for all 5 channels showing expected conversions tapering off as spend increases.
* **Key Bullet Points**:
  * **The Logarithmic Fatigue Model**: Models audience saturation using $f(x) = \alpha \ln(x+1)$.
  * **Marginal Efficiency**: As spend increases, conversion yield curves plateau, showing where ad budgets start losing power.
  * **Fitted Alpha Parameter**: Quantifies the scale-capacity of each channel.
* **Speaker Notes**:
  > *"We cannot assume returns are linear. Every channel suffers from audience ad fatigue. We modeled this using a logarithmic saturation curve. As you can see on the plot, after a certain spend threshold, adding more money to a channel yields very few extra conversions. Our optimizer finds the inflection points to prevent budget waste."*

## Slide 7: Portfolio Reallocation Blueprint
* **Canva Slide Title**: The 100 Crore Reallocation Blueprint
* **Visual Layout Idea**: A clear side-by-side table comparing the *Historical Budget* vs. *Optimized Budget* allocation per channel for next quarter. Highlight the **"+323% Expected Conversions Gain"** badge.
* **Key Bullet Points**:
  * **Portfolio Constraints**: Allocates a fresh ₹100 Crore portfolio (exactly ₹10 Crore per brand) with a ₹5 Crore cap per channel.
  * **Optimized Splits**: Redirects budgets from saturated channels (e.g. E-commerce) into high-marginal value primers (e.g. YouTube).
  * **Portfolio Impact**: Expected conversions scale from **4,134 to 17,504 (+323% Efficiency Gain)**.
* **Speaker Notes**:
  > *"By running a bounded SLSQP solver across our ₹100 Crore budget, we reallocate spend to maximize total conversions. Instead of simple scaling, the optimizer shifts budget away from fatiguing networks into high-marginal value slots, resulting in a massive 323% expected increase in conversions."*
